# Board Pitch: Securing MedDefense Health Systems
 
## Current State
MedDefense's current state is a vulnerable security posture: flat internal networks, shared staff login credentials, and outdated perimeter defenses form our baseline today. While we have basic digital systems in place to support clinical workflows, we currently lack critical safeguards like multi-factor authentication for remote access, internal network separation, and isolated backups. This leaves our patient records and core hospital infrastructure wide open to external cyber threats and lateral movement.
 
## The Risk
If we maintain our current posture, we remain an easy target for cybercriminals. A single ransomware attack that simultaneously disables our primary patient records and backup systems would result in an estimated $468,300 in immediate financial losses, regulatory fines, and operational downtime. More importantly, this would paralyze clinical care, divert emergency ambulances, and put patient safety at severe risk.
 
## The Plan
I request approval for a $120,000 capital investment to secure our core infrastructure. Instead of trying to fix everything at once, we will focus on three fundamental protections:
1. **Isolated Patient Data Backups**: Creating secure, tamper-proof backup copies that attackers cannot encrypt or delete, guaranteeing we can always restore operations without paying a ransom.
2. **Mandatory Identity Verification**: Requiring secondary approval codes (similar to modern banking logins) for every staff member accessing patient systems remotely, stopping unauthorized entry at the front door.
3. **Internal Digital Compartments**: Dividing our internal hospital network into secure zones so that if an intruder breaches one clinic or department, they are legally and digitally trapped and cannot spread to critical patient databases.
## The Return
This targeted investment will reduce our projected annual risk exposure by approximately $275,000. In business terms, we are spending $120,000 once to eliminate nearly three times that amount in recurring annual liabilities, safeguarding our hospital's financial health and ensuring uninterrupted care for our community.
