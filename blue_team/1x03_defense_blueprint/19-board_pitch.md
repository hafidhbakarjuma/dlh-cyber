# Board Pitch: Securing MedDefense

**Current State**
Following a comprehensive four-week assessment, our security posture is currently inadequate. We are operating with a flat network and lack essential safeguards, leaving the hospital exposed to preventable, high-impact cyberattacks. 

**The Risk**
If we do nothing, we remain a prime target for ransomware. A single incident—such as the destruction of our production and backup systems simultaneously—would result in an estimated $468,300 loss[cite: 1]. This would paralyze clinical operations, compromise patient safety, and result in catastrophic financial and reputational damage.

**The Plan**
I request approval for a $120,000 security investment. We will prioritize three core controls: implementing immutable offsite backups to guarantee recovery, deploying Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) to lock down remote access, and executing network segmentation to contain potential threats. These steps will create a resilient foundation, effectively breaking the most common "kill chains" used by attackers to move through our network and encrypt our critical data.

**The Return**
This investment will reduce our projected annual loss by approximately $275,000. Essentially, for every dollar spent, we are avoiding more than two dollars in future losses, while significantly hardening our ability to protect patient care and institutional integrity.
