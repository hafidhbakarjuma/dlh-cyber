# MedDefense Health Systems: Acceptable Use Policy (AUP)

## 1. Purpose and Scope
The purpose of this policy is to outline the rules for using MedDefense technology, networks, and data resources. This policy applies to all employees, contractors, vendors, and volunteers who have access to MedDefense information systems. This policy covers all electronic devices, software, and data owned or managed by MedDefense, including those used in clinical and administrative settings.

## 2. Acceptable Use of Systems
Employees are expected to use MedDefense systems primarily for authorized business and clinical purposes. Occasional, reasonable, and limited personal use is permitted provided it does not:
* Interfere with work duties or patient care.
* Consume excessive network bandwidth or storage.
* Violate any other MedDefense policy or legal requirement.
All users must act with professional judgment, as all activity on these systems may be subject to monitoring.

## 3. Prohibited Activities
To mitigate risks identified in our security risk assessment (e.g., RISK-003, RISK-005, RISK-007), the following activities are strictly forbidden:
* **Unauthorized Access**: Attempting to bypass security controls or accessing data for which you have no clinical or business necessity.
* **Malicious Software**: Knowingly introducing, downloading, or running unauthorized software or scripts.
* **Network Tampering**: Connecting unauthorized hardware (e.g., personal routers, Raspberry Pis) to the hospital network.
* **System Misuse**: Using hospital resources for illegal activities, harassment, or personal gain.

## 4. Personal Devices and Removable Media
Due to the risk of malware introduction and data exfiltration (e.g., RISK-004), the following rules apply:
* **Removable Media**: The use of personal USB drives or external storage devices on hospital workstations is prohibited unless explicitly approved by IT for a specific business task. 
* **Personal Devices**: Personal laptops may not be connected to the secure clinical network. Personal phones may only connect to the designated guest Wi-Fi and must not be used to store or transmit Patient Health Information (PHI).

## 5. Password and Authentication Requirements
To secure access to MedDefense resources, all users must adhere to the following:
* **MFA**: Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) must be used for all remote access and administrative accounts as per our security controls.
* **Credentials**: Passwords must be complex, unique, and never shared. Sharing credentials (e.g., shared PACS logins) is a direct violation of this policy and significantly increases organizational risk.
* **Session Security**: Always lock your terminal (Windows + L) when stepping away from a workstation to prevent unauthorized access.

## 6. Data Handling
All data must be handled according to its classification (e.g., PHI, Financial, or Public).
* **Patient Data (PHI)**: Must never be sent via unencrypted email or stored on unapproved personal devices.
* **Data Disposal**: Sensitive physical documents containing PHI must be placed in designated secure shredding bins, not standard trash cans.
* **Reporting**: Any suspected loss or exposure of data must be reported to the IT Security team immediately.

## 7. Monitoring and Enforcement
MedDefense reserves the right to monitor, log, and inspect any activity on its systems to ensure compliance with this policy, protect patient safety, and maintain system integrity. Violations of this policy will be investigated and may result in disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment or contract, and potential legal action.
